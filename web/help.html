Recording Software

Windows

If you have a Windows computer, you likely already have the Camera app, which can be used to record video. Any videos you create in the app will be saved under Pictures/Camera Roll.

Mac

Macs come with two good options for recording video: Photo Booth and QuickTime.

Zoom

If you have Zoom, you can also use it to record a video of just yourself. It takes a little more work than the other options listed on this page, but produces good results. You will need a (free) zoom account in order to record.

  1. Open Zoom and sign in if you are not already signed in.
  2. Important: make sure to change the default audio settings, which are designed for clear audio during video conferencing, but will make a singing recording sound strange.
    • Open Zoom's settings and select Audio.
    • Click Advanced.
    • Then check the box labled Show in-meeting option to "Enable Original Sound" from microphone.
  3. Now, start a new meeting. You won't invite anyone else to join this meeting, but you can only record in Zoom when you're in a meeting.
  4. You should see an option in the top left of your screen to Turn on Original Sound, which you enabled in step 2. Click that button. If the button says Turn off Original Sound instead, you don't need to change anything.
  5. Finally, turn on your video and audio and click the Record button on the bottom of your screen.
  6. When you are done recording, either click End Meeting (in the bottom right), or click Stop Recording, and then end the meeting. If you have successfully created a recording, you should see a window that says it is converting and saving the recording. You should see an .mp4 file with a video recording, and an .m4a file with just the audio.
